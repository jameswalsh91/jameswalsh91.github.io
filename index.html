My names James Walsh & I'm not from around here.

 

As an exchange student from Australia, TU/e has given me the chance to further my knowledge as a designer and discover new parts of the world while

undergoing my semester as a B2.2 student. I would like you to come with me now on a journey through time and space, to delve deeper into the vision, growth, and goals that have been realized and accomplished.

In my vision for the future I want to be able to walk down the street and have the walls surprise me with bright colors and visuals that are controlled by how fast I’m walking, my heart rate, or the music I’m listening to. I want roads and foot paths capable of telling me how fast I’m running; every step an explosion of color appearing as my foot collides with the surface. Every surface interacts with

everyone in some way or another, but these surfaces read your moods and respond in a way that excites and surprises the user.

Walking home late at night is no longer a frightening process as a walking friend is projected next to you. If you need directions, an illuminated path is made for you. If you’d like a surface to draw on, then why not use an entire facade of a building that is programmed to respond to creative output via an App on your phone, HUD or shared hallucinatory brain implant.

 

Tomorrow’s world has a wider range of dynamic installations created around town that give children and adults interactive playgrounds. Interactive singularities make people wonder about the potential of their everyday landscape, and

question “What is normal?".

 

Ultimately I want to see designers guide the public in creating stimulating, playful interactions with their community for the benefit of creating a response and a feeling which will define “community”.

 

Behavior such as intrigue, fascination and discovery are things most adults loose in the process of growing up. With age, expectations of the extraordinary typically diminish, and the environment around us becomes uniform in the terms of ordinary. Thought provoking designs challenge our expectations of the known, create shifts in belief and change a persons interpretation of their environment. Design will be able to culture-jam preconceptions; Sharp objects form out of soft materials, cold feels warm and frightening transforms into fascinating. The idea of challenging expectations takes the user back to their childhood, where learning what is good is bad and what is hot is cold, is re-imagined and relearned

according to whatever the corporate overlords decide it should be.

Growing up my focus has shifted between design, art and music. Today these are the elements that help drive my designs and give me inspiration. Having begun my studies in fine arts, I developed the need to create public reaction and emotion from my work. Initially I would create a drawing on paper, but felt that leaving these works in two dimensions was dull and lifeless to both the viewer and myself. The Realization that many industrial designers have at a young age is the need to transform the sketch into something tangible, and it was at this point through the first year of my fine arts course that i realized this, and made the decision to study design.

Design through the study of forms has been a great passion for me. Developing shapes through abstracting lines, planes and perspective are just a few of the methods that bring me great joy. Not only is there great elation from developing a vision into a sketch, and that sketch into a product, but a true joy of mine is the prototyping stage. Experimenting with materials and discovering the limits of what can be achieved is by far the most challenging and rewarding task. Having

developed a product from sketch to refined prototype in my previous semester at RMIT gave me great joy, but ultimately great respect for the work that designers do everyday in achieving not only beautiful and functional objects but innovations.

The designer that I will become not only creates objects and installations, but also creates experiences not found in the users everyday environment. Creating

artefact's and experiences that challenge expectations, provoke curiosity and tell a story are all elements that define beauty, and are what I’d like to master in my career.

 

 

So far the identity I have created for myself revolves around hand crafted products. These products are developed through form studies, and abstracted

interpretations of complex shapes. My workshop skills developed to a high

standard to the point that I can use a wide range of hand tools and machines in producing my prototypes and models. 3D modeling is a personal ability, with Solidworks as a skill and that I have developed over the past three years. I'm able to create just about any form and  ready it for diverse

manufacturing processes.

 

Yet recently a passion for interactive installations that question our expectations of our surrounding environment has lead me to come to Eindhoven as an exchange student. The zeitgeist of our time limits humanities communal and personal

fascination. Very little excites or surprises the average person on a daily basis, as they have become desensitized through constant attachment and interaction with electronic devices.  The time for pushing our assumptions and perceptions of the everyday environment is increasingly necessary to stimulate human

consciousness.

My short term goals while studying at TU/e is to develop my knowledge and skills in user centered design, as well as areas of creative electronics. These skills I feel would greatly further my ability to reach longterm goals of creating user centered

interactive installations that evoke emotions, that question our assumptions of our environment, and that spark a sense of imagination.

 

Developing knowledge in Arduino would be a great start for my Wearable Senses project, and would also unlock a library of possibilities with its open source platform.

More importantly finding out the possibilities of what these new technologies and electronics can do, and discover how they can be implemented in different ways to either excite, stimulate or even change our behavior inspired me.

With that being said my goals for the end of the semester is to develop a project that incorporates a technology that I

previously did not know existed. Programming the electronics will be difficult, but applying it in a clever and original way will be the greatest challenge.

 

Looking to my long term goals, I've realized that perhaps making nice looking products isn't enough. Creating an original

looking object that functions as a piece of furniture or lighting is becoming a perpetual cycle of repetition and regurgitation. The path of interactive design is the new kid on the block, and the potential for what can be achieved is still yet to be defined. Creating emotions and experiences is a greater satisfaction than observations, and by the time I complete my degree I hope to be able to create installations/products that evoke a strong connection to the user on an emotional level.
